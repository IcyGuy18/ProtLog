PTMKB provides streamlined access to comprehensive Post-Translational Modification data, tools, and algorithms to support research and innovation in drug discovery, disease understanding, and biotechnology.

Background

PTMKB is a knowledge base developed by BIRL that hosts information involving experimentally verified Post-Translational Modification (PTM) of proteins.

The researchers at BIRL present clean PTM data, with more than 200,000 proteins and over 70 different types of PTMs to explore. Along with the extensive amounts of data, we also provide a per-residue Propensity scoring metric that can be utilized for AI development and a RESTful API package that allows seamless integration of both features into systems and workflows. This is achieved with the help of various PTM and protein-related databases such as dbPTM, UniProt, and RCSB PDB Data Bank, amongst others. We provide access to download the data as well as the frequency tables used for Propensity calculation.

Let us know how PTMKB has helped in your research, or if you have questions about PTMKB in general, at safeeullah@lums.edu.pk.

Key Features

Search and find extensive detail on PTMs (including evidences, secondary structures, and solvent accessible surface area)
Use the Propensity Calculator to find the occurrence of a PTM on a residue based on its neighbouring residues
Integrate PTMKB into systems through a fully-featured RESTful API for seamless data access and automation
View and download the Propensity matrices for inclusion in your systems or scientific literature
